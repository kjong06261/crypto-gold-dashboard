üí∞ What is Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA)?

Dollar Cost Averaging is an investment strategy where you invest a fixed amount of money at regular intervals, regardless of the asset's price.

Example: Instead of investing $12,000 all at once, you invest $1,000 per month for 12 months.

‚úÖ Advantages of DCA

‚Ä¢ Reduces Timing Risk: You don't need to guess the perfect entry point

‚Ä¢ Emotional Discipline: Automates investing, removes emotion from decisions

‚Ä¢ Buy More When Low: Fixed dollar amount buys more shares when prices drop

‚Ä¢ Beginner Friendly: Simple strategy that doesn't require market expertise

‚Ä¢ Flexible Commitment: Start small and increase over time

‚ùå Disadvantages of DCA

‚Ä¢ Lower Returns in Bull Markets: Lump sum typically outperforms when markets rise consistently

‚Ä¢ More Transaction Fees: Multiple purchases can mean more fees (use commission-free brokers)

‚Ä¢ Cash Drag: Uninvested cash earns little while waiting to be deployed

‚Ä¢ Not Optimal Statistically: Studies show lump sum wins ~66% of the time historically

üéØ When to Use Each Strategy

Use Lump Sum If:

‚Ä¢ You have a large sum available now (inheritance, bonus, etc.)

‚Ä¢ You have a long time horizon (10+ years)

‚Ä¢ You can handle short-term volatility emotionally

‚Ä¢ Historical data favors this approach

Use DCA If:

‚Ä¢ You're investing from regular income (salary, business revenue)

‚Ä¢ You're worried about market timing and want to reduce regret

‚Ä¢ You're new to investing and want to ease in gradually

‚Ä¢ You value emotional comfort over statistical optimization

üí° Best Practice: Hybrid Approach

Many investors use a hybrid strategy:

1. Invest a portion immediately (50-80%)

2. DCA the remainder over 3-6 months

3. Continue regular monthly contributions from income

This balances statistical advantage with psychological comfort.
