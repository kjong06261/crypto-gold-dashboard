What is Market Capitalization?

Beginner Guide‚Ä¢4 min read

Market capitalization (or "market cap") is the total market value of a company's outstanding shares. It's one of the most basic ways to measure a company's size in the stock market.

1. How Market Cap is Calculated

Market Cap = Share Price √ó Shares Outstanding

For example, if a company has 100 million shares outstanding and the stock trades at $50 per share, its market cap is $5 billion.

Market cap changes constantly as the stock price moves. It represents what the market currently values the company at, not necessarily what the company is "worth" in any fundamental sense.

2. Size Classifications

üìä Common Categories

Large-Cap: Generally $10 billion or more. Often established, well-known companies.

Mid-Cap: Generally $2-10 billion. Often growing companies.

Small-Cap: Generally $300 million to $2 billion. Often younger or more volatile companies.

Micro-Cap: Generally under $300 million. Often very volatile and less liquid.

*These thresholds vary by source and change over time.

3. What Market Cap Tells You

Market cap gives a sense of relative size and is used to compare companies and construct indices. Large-cap stocks often receive more analyst coverage and tend to be more liquid (easier to buy and sell).

However, market cap doesn't tell you whether a stock is cheap or expensive. A $500 billion company isn't necessarily a better investment than a $5 billion company‚Äîit's just larger.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Limitation

Market cap reflects market sentiment, not intrinsic value. Two companies with identical market caps might have vastly different revenues, profits, debt levels, and growth prospects. Market cap is a starting point, not a complete picture.

4. Market Cap vs. Enterprise Value

Enterprise value (EV) is an alternative measure that adds debt and subtracts cash from market cap. Some analysts prefer EV because it better reflects what it would cost to actually acquire a company, including assuming its debts.

Conclusion

Market capitalization is a simple calculation that shows a company's total market value based on its current stock price. It's useful for classifying companies by size and comparing relative positions, but it has limitations and shouldn't be used alone to evaluate investments.
