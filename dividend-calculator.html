üìä Understanding Dividend Yield

Dividend Yield shows what percentage of the stock price is paid out as dividends annually. For example, a 4% yield means you receive $4 per year for every $100 invested.

Yield on Cost compares dividends to your original purchase price. If a stock's dividend grows over time, your yield on cost increases even if the current yield stays the same.
