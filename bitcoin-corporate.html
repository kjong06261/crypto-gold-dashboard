1. The FASB Catalyst: Real-Time Valuation

Prior to the new FASB (Financial Accounting Standards Board) amendments, corporations were forced to report Bitcoin as an intangible asset, meaning they had to record impairment charges if the price dropped but could not report gains until the asset was sold.

In 2026, firms now report their digital assets at Fair Market Value every quarter. This change has removed a massive psychological and accounting hurdle for CFOs, allowing companies like MicroStrategy (MSTR) and emerging tech firms to align their balance sheets with the real-time performance of the digital gold market.
