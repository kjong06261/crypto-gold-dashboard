2. On-Chain Analysis: Yield Sustainability and Network Security

In the digital asset ecosystem, "Yield" is not created out of thin air. It is a derivative of network transaction fees and inflationary rewards. To understand the sustainability of crypto dividends, one must look at On-chain Metrics such as the "Staking Ratio" and "Net Flow of Institutional Wallets."

For Ethereum, the transition to Proof-of-Stake has created a deflationary pressure mechanism (EIP-1559). As network activity increases, more ETH is burned than issued, effectively increasing the value of the staked assets. This is fundamentally different from a Yield Trap in the stock market; it is an algorithmic monetary policy that rewards long-term holders while maintaining the security of the global decentralized computer.

3. Risk Management: Navigating Volatility and Smart Contract Risks

While the rewards are attractive, crypto staking is not without risk. Investors must account for Slashing Risks (penalties for validator downtime) and liquidity lock-up periods. A sophisticated passive income strategy should utilize "Liquid Staking" protocols (e.g., Lido, Rocket Pool) to maintain asset liquidity while earning rewards.
