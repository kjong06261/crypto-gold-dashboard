Market Analysis Methodology

The Nasdaq-100 Index (NDX) represents a modified capitalization-weighted index of 100 of the largest non-financial companies listed on the Nasdaq stock exchange. For institutional investors and independent traders, understanding the technical structure of NDX is crucial for gauging the sentiment of the broader technology sector, particularly in the fields of Artificial Intelligence (AI), semiconductors, and cloud computing.

Key Technical Indicators to Watch

When utilizing this terminal, traders should focus on specific technical indicators that historically correlate with Nasdaq volatility:

  • Moving Averages (MA): The 50-day and 200-day moving averages serve as critical support and resistance levels. A "Golden Cross" often signals bullish momentum.
  • RSI (Relative Strength Index): An RSI above 70 typically indicates an overbought condition, while below 30 suggests oversold territory.
  • Volume Profile: analyzing volume at specific price levels helps identify institutional accumulation or distribution zones.

Macro-Economic Correlation

The valuation of growth stocks within the Nasdaq-100 is highly sensitive to Federal Reserve interest rate policies. As the discount rate for future cash flows changes, the intrinsic value of high-growth tech companies fluctuates. This dashboard integrates real-time price action to help investors visualize these macroeconomic impacts instantly.
