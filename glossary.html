Terminology Masterlist

Empower your investment strategy with deep insights into both fundamental and technical analysis indicators used by institutional researchers.

Accumulation/Distribution Line (A/D Line)
A volume-based technical indicator designed to measure the cumulative flow of money into and out of a security. It confirms price trends and spots potential reversals by identifying divergences between price and volume flow.
Bollinger Bands
A volatility indicator consisting of a middle SMA and two standard deviation bands. Narrowing bands (Squeezes) often precede explosive price movements, while touches of the upper/lower bands can signal overbought or oversold conditions.
Dividend Aristocrats
An elite tier of S&P 500 companies that have increased their dividend payouts for at least 25 consecutive years. They represent the gold standard of defensive, income-generating equity assets.
Fibonacci Retracement
A tool used to identify support and resistance levels based on mathematical ratios (23.6%, 38.2%, 61.8%). Institutional traders use these levels to find high-probability entry points after a major trend.
MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
A momentum oscillator that tracks the relationship between two EMAs. Signal line crossovers are used as primary triggers for trend-following entries and exits.
RSI (Relative Strength Index)
A momentum oscillator measuring the speed and change of price moves. Readings above 70 indicate overbought conditions, while readings below 30 signal oversold conditions.
