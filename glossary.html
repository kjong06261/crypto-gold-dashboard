Tools & Indicators Glossary

Accumulation/Distribution Line (A/D Line) A volume-based indicator designed to measure the cumulative flow of money into and out of a security. It helps traders confirm trends by comparing price movements with volume.
Bollinger Bands A volatility indicator consisting of a moving average (simple) and two standard deviation lines plotted above and below the moving average. When the bands tighten, it signals a period of low volatility.
Dividend Aristocrats S&P 500 companies that have increased their dividend payouts for at least 25 consecutive years. These are considered defensive assets during recessionary periods.
Fibonacci Retracement A technical tool used to identify potential support and resistance levels based on key numbers (23.6%, 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%). Traders use these levels to place entry and exit orders.
Golden Cross A bullish breakout pattern formed from a crossover involving a security's short-term moving average (such as the 50-day moving average) breaking above its long-term moving average (such as the 200-day moving average).
Heatmap (Market Map) A data visualization technique that shows the relative size (Market Cap) and performance (Color) of stocks in a single glance. Green indicates positive performance, while red indicates negative.
MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) A trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages of a security’s price. It is calculated by subtracting the 26-period EMA from the 12-period EMA.
RSI (Relative Strength Index) A momentum oscillator that measures the speed and change of price movements. RSI oscillates between zero and 100. Traditionally, RSI is considered overbought when above 70 and oversold when below 30.
